SHAPE:

After completing SSP, I had the privilege of attending yet another great summer program: the Summer High School Academic Program for Engineers (SHAPE)

in Electrical Engineering. The program was held at Columbia University. My peers (in total, about 25 students) and I were taught the physics behind electrodynamics.

We were split into groups of about 4 people, in which we worked with breadboards, LED lights, system controllers, etc., which we coded using C++. I was the main coder,

documenter, and engineer and did the main calculations. We built a solar oven, which is displayed below. We measured the temperature using a thermocouple, which we

created using our materials. The sun would heat the solar, which would direct its heat to the black trash bag.

After building the solar oven, we baked some cookies, as displayed below. Our oven reached approximately 60 Celsius inside. Our thermocouple is also

displayed below:

After the solar oven project, we designed a theoretical kiosk project, which I documented with its calculations, models, etc. Our idea was to make a

trolley system around the Columbia campus that would run on solar panels. My team and I also made some CAD designs for the trolley and the railway. The link

to the document is displayed in the "Links" section.

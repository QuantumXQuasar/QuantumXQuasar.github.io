Trebuchet:

This is one of the lighter projects I did in my high school career. At the start of my 12th grade, I created a Trebuchet made of cardboard. The arm was a

modified pencil. The sling was made of cardboard. All the materials were put together with a hot glue gun and some tape. The arm holder was also a modified

pencil. Strings would apply the potential energy to the arm. I used a motor with its servo arm to contain this potential energy and keep the arm at rest.

This motor was connected to the system controller, which was coded in Java. I coded the buttons so that one button would turn the servo arm, letting go of the arm,

converting its potential energy to kinetic, and shooting its payload. Another would turn the servo arm so the trebuchet arm would stay in one place.

The payload that the trebuchet would shoot would be ping pong balls. The trebuchet shot ping pong balls at a distance of approximately 7 meters.

SSP:

In the summer after my junior year of High School, I experienced the golden moments of the Summer Science program. I completed the program in

Astrophysics, where a cohort of 36 students, including me, participated in 39 days of enlightening research experience. We were separated into groups

of three, and each team was assigned its own unique asteroid to research. My team and I used a research-grade telescope to observe the asteroid (ours

was 1866 Sysiphus). After, we used image reduction to figure out the right ascension and the decline of the asteroid (basically latitude and longitude

but for the celestial sphere rather than Earth). Next, we determined the sun-asteroid velocity and position vector of our asteroid using the method of

Gauss â€“ the famous approximation method, which approximates the 3 axis of the asteroid-sun's velocity and position vector. After determining the velocity

and position vector, we used more complex equations to assess 6 orbital elements of the asteroid: The longitude of the ascending node, inclination, eccentricity,

argument of perihelion, the semi-major axis, and the mean anomaly.

Apart from the research, we also attended lectures held by professors. Professors taught us multivariable calculus and linear algebra-based

physics, astronomy, and Python packages such as numpy and matplotlib. We also collaborated on Psets, which were so challenging that they made us collaborate

so much and utilize each other's unique abilities to complete them. Psets consisted of Linear algebra and Multivariable calculus math and physics questions,

astronomy questions about how telescopes work, CCDs, and usage of celestial coordinates; moreover, we used our math skills in coding the formulas with numpy

and plotting the data with matplotlib. Outside work, we played pool and table tennis, had a talent show, played tag, and went on field trips.

SSP was one of the best experiences of my life. I was surrounded by Geniuses, and we developed a strong bond that lasts to this day. The research,

the Psets, and all the fun activities we did together were indeed one of a kind. If I could wipe my memory and go back in time, I would do it all over again.
